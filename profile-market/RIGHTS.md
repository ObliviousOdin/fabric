# Rights and attribution

Fabric Profile Market combines original personas, public-domain inspirations,
and two unofficial fan-made collections.

## DC and Marvel fan profiles

The `dc-universe` and `marvel-universe` shelves are unofficial fan works. The
names of their fictional universes and characters, and the underlying
characters and settings, belong to their respective rights holders. This
project is not affiliated with, sponsored by, approved by, or endorsed by DC,
Marvel, Warner Bros. Discovery, Disney, or their affiliates.

Those profiles contain behavioral descriptions written in original language.
They contain no franchise logos, comic pages, promotional art, film or
television stills, actor likenesses, costume reproductions, scripts, episode or
issue transcripts, copied dialogue, catchphrases, music, audio, or video.

The project's contributor MIT grant covers its new code and original prose;
adapted generic scaffolding remains under the upstream MIT notice reproduced
in `THIRD_PARTY_NOTICES.md`. Neither grant conveys rights in third-party names,
characters, settings, trademarks, or other protected material. An unofficial
or non-commercial label is not a substitute for permission. Obtain qualified
rights review before commercial distribution, paid promotion, merchandising,
or branding that relies on these shelves.

## Public-domain inspirations

`mythic-council`, `round-table`, `baker-street-bureau`, and `gothic-cabinet`
draw on public-domain myths and literature. The generated text is new; it does
not copy a modern adaptation, translation, illustration, performance, film,
television series, game, or other later expression. Some modern adaptations
may have independent rights even when the underlying source is public domain.

## Original collections

`galactic-expedition`, `arcane-academy`, `neon-circuit`, and
`creator-studio` are original role collections. Similarities to particular
people or proprietary fictional characters are unintended.

## No identity claim

Every profile is a behavioral style for Fabric. It must never claim to be a
fictional or historical person, possess that person's memories or
relationships, or exercise their authority. Character and role labels are
discovery aids, not claims about sentience, identity, credentials, or
affiliation.
